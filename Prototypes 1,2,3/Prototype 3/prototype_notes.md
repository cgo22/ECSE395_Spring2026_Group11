# Prototype: Low Fidelity Size and Layout Prototype

## Objective
The goal of Prototype 1 was to test the physical size constraints and internal layout of the pill dispensing device. This prototype evaluates whether a cylindrical housing with approximate dimensions of 4 in × 4 in × 11 in (diameter × diameter × height) can accommodate four pill storage compartments.

This prototype focuses only on spatial layout and size constraints and does not include any electronics or dispensing mechanisms.

## Design Dimensions
Approximate prototype dimensions:

- Diameter: ~4 inches
- Height: ~11 inches

These dimensions come from the current system specification being explored by the team.

The diameter is particularly important because it affects portability. A 4-inch diameter device may already be somewhat thick for portability, while also forcing the internal pill compartments and electronics to remain very slim.

Further testing will be required to determine the optimal diameter for the device.

## Construction Method
The prototype was constructed using simple materials to quickly test the geometry and internal layout.

Outer Shell
- The cylindrical shell was created using a piece of notebook divider paper (thicker than standard notebook paper).
- The paper was rolled into a cylindrical shape and secured with staples.

Internal Compartments
- Four pill compartments were created using rolled pieces of standard notebook paper.
- Each rolled paper tube represents one pill storage compartment.

Internal Divider Structure
- Two pieces of cardboard were used to create a cross-shaped divider.
- One cardboard piece had a slit cut in the center so the second piece could slide into it, forming a cross configuration.
- This cross divider splits the cylinder into four sections.

Assembly
- The cardboard divider was attached to the outer shell using tape.
- The rolled paper tubes were attached to each other and to the divider using staples.
- Staples were also used to help secure the tubes to the shell structure.

## Electronics Compartment
This prototype does not include a central electronics compartment due to the limitations of the crude prototype materials.

However, the intended design includes a fifth central compartment that would contain the electronic components, such as:

- ESP32 microcontroller
- Sensors
- Battery and power components

This electronics compartment would be located in the center of the four pill compartments.

## Observations
This prototype demonstrates that four pill compartments can fit within the proposed cylindrical housing.

However, several important constraints became apparent:

- The 4-inch diameter significantly limits internal space.
- Fitting both pill compartments and electronics may require extremely slim internal components.
- The internal layout may need to be redesigned to accommodate a central electronics chamber.

Because of these constraints, additional prototypes will likely explore different internal arrangements and potentially different overall device diameters.

## Future Mechanical Features
The current prototype focuses only on the structural layout.

The full device is expected to include additional mechanical features such as:

- A knob mechanism on the bottom of the device to dispense pills
- A lid or cap on the top of the device to seal the pill compartments
- A central compartment to house electronic components

These components would add to the overall device height and must be accounted for in future prototypes.

## Key Question Tested
Can four pill compartments fit inside a cylindrical device with approximate dimensions of 4 in diameter and 11 in height?

## Next Steps
Future prototypes will focus on:

- Testing integration of a central electronics compartment
- Testing sensor integration and electronics placement
